Scope of Work

Web Design
Positions P&L Curve

Seeing the whole story, at a glance

By the end of this project, traders no longer had to interpret scattered numbers to understand their performance.

Instead, they could look at a single screen and instantly answer:

  • How is my trade performing right now?

  • Where did it go right—or wrong?

  • Am I within my risk limits?

What used to take multiple steps—switching between tables, calculations, and mental tracking—was reduced to a single, continuous visual experience.

What changed

The experience shifted from:

  • Static numbers → Visual movement over time

  • Fragmented data → A single, unified view

  • Interpretation → Instant understanding

At the center of this shift was a P&L curve—a graph that made performance intuitive, even for someone unfamiliar with trading.

Around it, key elements added clarity:

  • Risk boundaries through stop-loss and target lines

  • Real-time insights through a dedicated analytics panel

  • Context through best/worst performers and key metrics

Together, this created something more than a feature—
a live “health view” of a trader’s positions.

Why this needed to exist

Before this, traders had access to all the right data—but not in a way that was easy to understand.

Performance was spread across tables and numbers, making it hard to:

  • Track how a trade evolved during the day

  • Identify critical moments of profit or loss

  • Understand risk without manual effort

The system answered what the numbers were, but not what they meant.

That gap became the foundation of this project.

Designing for clarity, not complexity

The goal was not to add more information—but to make existing information effortless to understand.

The solution centered around a simple idea:

Turn trading data into a visual narrative.

This led to:

  • A P&L curve showing profit and loss over time

  • Visual markers for key decision points (entry, stop loss, target)

  • Layered information architecture—graph first, details later

Every decision followed one principle:
“A user should understand their position within seconds.”

Balancing beginners and experts

One of the key challenges was designing for two very different users:

  • Someone new to trading

  • Someone who trades actively every day

The solution was to layer the experience:

  • The graph provides instant clarity (no expertise needed)

  • The analytics panel provides depth (for advanced users)

  • The data table ensures precision (for validation and detail)

This way, the interface doesn’t overwhelm beginners—
but still respects the needs of experienced traders.

The shift in experience

With this approach, the interaction changed fundamentally.

Users no longer:

  • Scan through rows of numbers

  • Mentally reconstruct what happened

  • Jump between multiple sections

Instead, they:

  • See performance as it unfolds

  • Recognize patterns instantly

  • Make faster, more confident decisions
