UX That Simplifies Retirement Planning

Making NPS jargon - FATCA, PRAN, Tier 2 Account feel like a friendly word.

>> Overview

“Wait... What is NPS?”

That’s the vibe we got from most people when we first started working on this flow.

At PensionBox, we wanted to help users plan for retirement in a simple, confident way. But the process was full of government terms, long forms, and confusing steps.

So our goal was simple:


  • “Let’s make it feel easy, safe, and quick — even for someone doing it for the first time.”

>> What Was the Problem?

What users were struggling with

You get dropped into 10+ form fields, redirected to random partner sites (hi, KFintech 👋), and are told to enter things like “FATCA declaration” without any clue what that even is.

So before doing anything design-wise, we decided to talk to people.

>> Talking to Users (All Kinds

Before designing, we needed to listen

We grouped users into 3 levels:

  • Level 0 — Never heard of NPS

  • Level 1 — Aware but never invested

  • Level 2 — Actively using NPS through Groww, KFintech, etc.

>> What We Changed

We needed a flow that guides, not confuses

The answers were blunt and golden:

  • “I didn’t even know I had to declare anything.”

  • “I got scared and just closed the tab.”

  • “Too many steps. And I didn’t know which one was safe.”

    Once we understood our users and mapped the problems, we needed to visually structure the journey a user would take to open an NPS account inside PensionBox.

>> What We Designed (and Why)

Here’s what actually made the difference:

  1. Step-by-step onboarding:

    The old onboarding screens showed long forms with 10+ fields at once, no progress indicator, and no sense of direction. Users didn’t know how many steps were left, or if they were even doing it right.

  1. Trust Boxes & Friendly UX Writing:

    Users were getting nervous when asked to enter personal data like PAN, Aadhaar, or make payments — especially when redirected to unfamiliar domains like KFintech.

    Government-style language like “Proceed to FATCA Declaration” or “Generate PRAN” was confusing and intimidating.

  1. Smart Form Design:

    Users were abandoning forms because they were long, not mobile-friendly, or gave no validation feedback.

  1. Save & Resume Later:

    NPS onboarding takes time. Some users dropped off mid-way and had to start over — frustrating experience.

>> Post Launch

15+ Micro Fixes That Make Everything Feel Smoother

The launch wasn't the end — it was just phase one. We continuously improved the experience based on real user feedback, friction logs, and observed drop-offs.

>> Final Outcomes

After Launch: What Changed

  • Drop-offs at payment and FATCA stages reduced significantly

  • Users completed onboarding in fewer attempts

  • People mentioned the process felt “safe and understandable”

  • Internal support tickets for NPS issues went down

>> Thank you!

The biggest takeaway? Small things—like a tooltip or trust badge—can have massive impact when placed with intent.

This project taught me how tiny trust cues can make or break a complex flow — and how simplicity is often hidden behind hundreds of design decisions.
